# GTM Plan

The exact target user for this tool is an engineering manager or technical founder at a 10-80 person startup, typically Series A to early Series B, where AI spending is decentralized. In these teams, engineers buy Cursor, Copilot, Claude, ChatGPT, and API credits independently. Finance sees only a monthly card aggregate and rarely gets per-seat or per-use-case clarity. This user cares about speed first, then efficiency, and only investigates spend when monthly burn suddenly jumps.

The highest-intent trigger moments are practical, not aspirational. Right before using this tool, they are usually searching phrases like: "cursor vs copilot pricing for team", "reduce claude api bill", "chatgpt team vs enterprise", "how to audit ai tool spend startup", or reading X threads about "AI bill shock". They also arrive from internal Slack messages like "why are we spending $4k on AI subscriptions?" and from monthly finance review docs where software line items spike.

The channels are specific founder/operator communities rather than broad social posting:
- X lists focused on SaaS operators, devtool founders, and indie hackers.
- r/startups, r/SaaS, r/ExperiencedDevs, and r/Entrepreneur where cost optimization threads already exist.
- Indie Hackers and small founder Slack groups where operators share tooling stacks.
- Product Hunt upcoming pages and launch communities where founder tools spread quickly.

First 100 users in 30 days with $0 paid budget:
1. Publish a transparent teardown thread: "We audited 20 startup AI stacks: median waste $X." Include 3 anonymized screenshots and the free tool link.
2. Post one high-signal case study per week on X + LinkedIn with exact before/after monthly spend math.
3. DM 30 founder/operator contacts with a one-line offer: "I built a free AI spend audit, happy to run yours live in 10 minutes."
4. Submit to 3 communities weekly (Indie Hackers, one subreddit, one Slack) with explicit rules compliance and no spam tone.
5. Add a share-link CTA inside the product so each completed audit can be posted publicly with PII removed.

The unfair distribution channel is Credex itself: once a few real audits show verified savings, Credex can publish anonymized benchmark snapshots from inbound audits (tool mix, spend bands, savings ranges). Competitors without that inbound deal flow cannot replicate this trust loop quickly.

Week-1 traction if this works:
- 150 unique visitors
- 45 completed audits (30% completion)
- 18 lead captures (40% post-result capture)
- 6 high-savings leads (>$500/mo)
- 2 booked consultations

These are small but meaningful numbers for an early B2B lead-gen product and enough signal to decide whether to double down on distribution or refine messaging.
