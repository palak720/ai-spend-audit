# Economics

To estimate unit economics, start with lead value. Suppose Credex earns blended net revenue of $2,500 per converted customer per year from AI credit resale margin and recurring account expansion. If the average retained lifetime is 2 years, expected gross lifetime value is about $5,000 per converted customer.

Now map funnel stages from this free audit:
- Audit completed -> consultation booked
- Consultation booked -> credit purchase

Assume realistic early funnel rates:
- 12% of completed audits book a consultation
- 30% of consultations convert to purchase
So conversion from audit to purchase is 0.12 x 0.30 = 3.6%.

Expected value per completed audit = 0.036 x $5,000 = $180.
This means each completed audit is economically valuable even before optimization.

CAC by channel (organic-heavy early phase):
1. Founder social case-study posts: mostly time cost, estimated CAC $40-$90 depending on conversion quality.
2. Community postings (Indie Hackers/Reddit/Slack): CAC $20-$70 if content is useful and non-promotional.
3. Warm outbound DMs to founder/operator network: CAC $60-$150 due to manual effort but typically higher close rates.

Profitability threshold check:
If expected value per completed audit is $180, a channel remains attractive while effective cost per completed audit is below ~$120 (leaving room for tooling, support, and failure cases). If cost rises above that for 4 consecutive weeks, that channel should be deprioritized.

What conversion rates make the tool profitable?
Let LTV per converted customer be $5,000.
Break-even conversion from audit to purchase at CAC $100 per completed audit:
$100 / $5,000 = 2.0% audit-to-purchase.
Current modeled conversion is 3.6%, which clears break-even with margin.

Path to $1M ARR in 18 months (rough but explicit):
- Target annual revenue/customer = $2,500
- Needed active customers for $1M ARR = 1,000,000 / 2,500 = 400 customers
- With 3.6% audit-to-purchase conversion, required completed audits = 400 / 0.036 ˜ 11,111 over 18 months
- Monthly completed audits needed ˜ 617

This is ambitious but plausible if distribution compounds through shareable audits, benchmark content, and operator referrals.

Key sensitivity assumptions:
- If purchase conversion drops from 30% to 20%, audit-to-purchase falls to 2.4% and required monthly audits rise materially.
- If blended annual revenue/customer is only $1,800, the volume requirement increases by ~39%.
- If consultation booking improves from 12% to 18%, economics improve sharply without changing traffic.

Conclusion: this tool is viable if it consistently creates qualified, high-savings consultations, not just vanity traffic.
